Fewer than 4,000 tigers in the wild

The demand for tigers and their parts is pushing these animals closer to extinction.

Originally there were nine subspecies of tigers, but over the last 80 years three have become completely extinct. The remaining six species of tigers are all considered endangered or critically endangered. The Bengal tiger, the most numerous of the subspecies, still only boasts a wild population of around 2,500.

what is happening

Across their range, tigers face unrelenting pressures from poaching, retaliatory killings, and habitat loss. They are forced to compete for space with dense and often growing human populations.
